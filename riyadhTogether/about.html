Envisioning taxi sharing from Riyadh's Union Station

RiyadhTOGETHER

RiyadhTOGETHER explores the potential of sharing taxi or ride-hail trips as a first-/last-mile solution for Riyadh's upcoming public transportation system.

New York City as a jumping board

As Riyadh's public transport system is still in construction, we conduct the exercise based on a city with existing public transport network, stations, and publically-available taxi trip data. We use New York City as a jumping board, with aims to develop tools and methods that can be applied to Riyadh.

Sharing works!

About 50% of the taxi trips from the studied station (Flushing-Main Street, New York City) can be shared with a trip time delay < 10 minutes for the passengers.

Trip-sharing is most feasible during the evening and night, when taxi ridership is the highest, leading to more opportunites for sharing.

Detailed Findings

How do we do it?

We process the data using R, compute the routing and shareability of trips using Google Maps Directions API and Mapzen's Valhalla in Python, and visualize the findings in D3 and CartoDB.
